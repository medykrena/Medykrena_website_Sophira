Why Data Science for Adults in Transition?

Published on July 25, 2025

In an economy increasingly driven by data and artificial intelligence, the ability to collect, analyse and interpret information has become essential. These skills are no longer reserved for engineers or large corporations – they are relevant to every sector, from healthcare and finance to energy, administration, retail and culture. For adults seeking a fresh start, data science offers a gateway to well‑paid and meaningful jobs.

A growing demand for data skills

Employers across Switzerland and Europe are seeking professionals who can make sense of the torrent of information generated each day. According to industry reports, roles such as data analyst, data scientist and full‑stack developer are among the top ten jobs of the future【302319168459486†L74-L85】. Salaries reflect the shortage of talent: developers earn about 6 500–8 000 CHF per month, cybersecurity engineers 7 000–9 000 CHF and data analysts or scientists around 6 800–8 500 CHF【414222387359247†L135-L155】. Meanwhile, the country could face a shortfall of 40 000 IT specialists by 2030【414222387359247†L135-L155】. Data‑driven roles are not limited to technology companies; they are needed in healthcare, finance, logistics, energy and even social services【302319168459486†L46-L64】【302319168459486†L74-L85】.

Why adults should consider this path

Data science appeals to those who enjoy problem‑solving, critical thinking and storytelling through numbers. For adults in transition, it provides several advantages:

  • Transferable skills: Many adults already use spreadsheets, databases or project management tools in their jobs. Learning to code or analyse data builds upon these foundations.
  • Flexibility: Data roles often allow remote or hybrid work, supporting a better balance between professional and personal life【302319168459486†L119-L128】.
  • Cross‑sector mobility: Data skills are applicable in diverse industries, offering resilience if one sector experiences a downturn.
  • Continuous learning: The field encourages lifelong learning, something familiar to Swiss adults – around 60 % engage in continuing education each year【213318533845871†L118-L127】.

Accessible training options

Entering the data world does not necessarily require a university degree in computer science. Modern curricula are modular and practice oriented. Sophira works with partners to provide step‑by‑step pathways:

  • The ICT‑Assistant programme delivers a foundation in computing over 800 hours and is open to newcomers【863326897908625†L139-L164】.
  • The Digital Collaboration Specialist course teaches change management and innovation skills and culminates in a federal diploma【863326897908625†L170-L194】.
  • The Cyber 4 Switzerland initiative prepares participants for the federal cybersecurity exam through online learning, a bootcamp and a work placement【863326897908625†L200-L223】.
  • Swissmem’s pilot programme offers modular training for adults with technical backgrounds wishing to pivot into new industrial roles【605013251932007†L69-L87】.

Thanks to advances in online education, around 30 % of continuing education in Switzerland is now delivered in hybrid or fully online formats【213318533845871†L137-L141】. This makes it easier for adults to learn while working or caring for family.

Building a supportive community

Pursuing a new career can be daunting, especially when learning complex subjects like statistics or programming. That is why Sophira emphasises community. We organise study groups, peer mentoring and networking events so that participants can share experiences and overcome obstacles together. We also encourage partnerships with employers who value diversity of backgrounds and life experience.

Take the leap

The demand for data skills will only grow. By investing in this field now, adults in transition can secure stable employment and play a role in shaping a more sustainable and equitable economy. Sophira is here to guide you on this journey – we believe that data literacy is a new form of empowerment. Join us to discover courses, mentors and a community that will help you thrive in the digital age.
